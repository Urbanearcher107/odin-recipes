Neapolitan Pizza

Wood fired Neapolitan style margherita pizza

Neapolitan pizza is simple yet delicious. Made with just a few high quality ingredients cooked to perfection. The final result is light and fresh pizza that tastes great.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups bread flour, or more as needed
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • Cooking spray, or olive oil
  • 1 14-ounce can whole peeled San Marzano tomatoes
  • 12 ounces fresh mozzarella, cut into 1/4-inch slices or torn into small chunks
  • 2 ounces fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil, divided

Steps

    Make the dough
  1. Combine the flour, salt, and yeast in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a dough hook attachment. Mix to combine.
  2. Add the water and mix to combine.
  3. Knead the dough on low speed for 10 minutes. The mixture should come together into a unified mass that barely sticks to the bottom of the bowl as it kneads. If the dough is sticking, add more flour, 1 tablespoon at a time, to the mixer while it is running, until the mass barely sticks to the bowl. If the mixture is too dry, add water, 1 tablespoon at a time, to the running mixer.
  4. Cover the bowl tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 8-72 hours.
  5. Transfer the dough to a floured work surface and divide into 4 balls.
  6. Let dough balls rise at room tempature for 2 hours.
    Make the sauce
  1. Pulse the tomatoes in a food processor until a chunky liquid is formed
    Assemble the pizza
  1. Heat the oven to at least 475 F higher if your oven allows.
  2. Onto a well-floured surface, stretch out each ball of dough into a 10-inch circle. Use a floured rolling pin if you need to. Don't worry about the dough being a perfect circle.
  3. Lightly flour a pizza peel making sure to cover where the dough will sit. Place one rolled-out piece of dough onto the peel and shake gently to ensure that the dough is not sticking.
  4. Spread about 1/3 cup of sauce over the dough, leaving about a 1/2-inch of the edge bare.
  5. Top the sauce with 1/4 of the mozzarella slices. Tear a few basil leaves and place them on top (according to your liking), and drizzle with 1 tablespoon of the olive oil. You can also add the basil and olive oil after the pizza is cooked.
  6. Transfer the pizza from peel to the hotstone
  7. Bake until the cheese is bubbly and the crust is charred
  8. Repeat with remaining dough and enjoy.
